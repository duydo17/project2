1.History

The bridge was designed by British architect Norman Foster and French bridge engineer Michel Virlogeux. This is the bridge that was once considered the tallest in the world and is still the bridge with the tallest pylon in the world. 2.Construction

The bridge construction process was extremely difficult and complicated when engineers and workers had to cope with factors such as landslides, gusts of wind over 130 km/h and big storms. Even the bridge's creator, engineer Michel Virlogeux, confessed "when I gave the first design of the bridge to the authorities, they thought I was crazy". Millau is also considered one of the greatest construction works of all time. The bridge also received the 2006 Best Construction Award from the International Association of Bridge and Structural Engineers.
